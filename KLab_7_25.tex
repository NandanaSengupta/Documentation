\frametitle{Cellphone Data from Boston}

\begin{itemize} \small

\item<1-> Work from Human Mobility and Networks Lab at MIT -- PI Marta Gonsalez 

\item<2-> {\color{red}Paper:} ``TimeGeo: a spatiotemporal framework for modeling urban mobility without surveys " (2016) -- Yang et al. 

\item<3-> {\color{red}Data:}  billions of geo-tagged mobile phone call records, made by millions of users.

\item<4-> {\color{red}Findings:}
\begin{itemize}  \small
\item<5->  methodological: assign labels to locations (home, work, other) 
\item<6-> provide origin destination matrices without expensive surveys
\end{itemize}



\item<7-> {\color{red}Open Question:} What explains how individuals travel between different location types? What characterizes places where individuals cluster or linger?



\end{itemize}
